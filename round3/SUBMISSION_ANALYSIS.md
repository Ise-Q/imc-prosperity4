# Round 3 — Official Submission Analysis

Comprehensive review of all 8 official submissions (`round3/logs/{389010, 389093, 390671, 390764, 390796, 390887, 390935, 390966}`). Each log dir holds the platform `.json` results, the `.log` execution trace, and the `.py` source actually uploaded.

Every official run was executed by the Prosperity platform on a **single day (day=2)** of historical data, ~1,000 sandbox records (~10,000 activity rows per product). For pre-submission EDA context, see `round3/notebooks/output/eda_*` (driven by `Base_EDA.ipynb`); the per-run plot artifacts in `round3/notebooks/output/results_*` were generated by `analyze_results.ipynb` against a single `LOG_DIR` and are point-in-time, so cross-run comparisons here come from a fresh sweep (`/tmp/analyze_round3.py`) over all eight logs.

- **VEV_5000–VEV_5500** — liquid call strikes, limit 300 each. Historical-data trade counts: 1, 1, 18, 121, 225, 267 (per EDA day-2 stats).
- **VEV_6000 / VEV_6500** — pinned at mid=0.5, illiquid; flagged `DEAD_VOUCHERS` in every strategy and skipped.

## 5. Strategy-by-strategy: Intent vs Execution

### 389010 — trader2 (baseline, +503)

- **Intent**: HYDROGEL passive MM (small size, no take). Vouchers: take only when mispriced; deep-ITM intrinsic arb. VEE: actively hedge aggregate option delta to neutral, market-order if needed.
- **Execution**: 9 HYDROGEL fills @ +6.56 ticks/fill avg edge → +627 (clean wins on the wide spread). 11+9 voucher fills locking in intrinsic on 4000/4500 → +325. VEE hedger fired 61 own trades at avg edge **−2.08 ticks** (paying the 5-tick spread heavily) → **−449**.
- **Verdict**: Logic ran as designed. The hedger is doing exactly what trader2's docstring claims it does, including the documented downside: chasing delta with market orders eats VEE spread.

### 389093 — trader2 + 0.1× hedger + VEE residual MM (+273)

- **Intent (inferred from diff)**: cut hedge sizing to 10%, add a passive residual MM quote on VEE to offset the spread cost.
- **Execution**: 117 VEE fills @ −1.32 avg edge — twice the volume of trader2 with smaller per-trade loss but bigger gross loss (**−679 VEE**). The residual MM filled but at the wrong time; reduced hedge sizing meant unhedged delta drifted further before each correction.
- **Verdict**: 1.5× more volume + same direction-of-loss → **net regression vs trader2**. Confirms that hedge sizing and residual MM are coupled — you can't half-tune one without re-tuning the other.

### 390671 — trader3 (full-size HYDROGEL + always-on liquid MM, **−6492**)

- **Intent**: capture the wide HYDROGEL spread at 100% size; MM all four liquid strikes always-on around BS fair; keep ITM arb.
- **Execution**:
  - HYDROGEL: 91 fills @ **−6.66 avg edge**. Position pinned at **+200 (full long limit)** at peak — clearest possible adverse-selection signature. **−6,215 PnL**.
  - VEV_5000: 5 fills, +26 PnL — trivial. VEV_5100/5200/5300/5400/5500: **0 own trades**, despite the always-on MM block.
  - Hit position limit on HYDROGEL → all subsequent buy orders for the product cancelled until the position rebuilt budget.
- **Verdict**: HYDROGEL full-size MM is fundamentally broken inside a 16-tick spread that has 96% AC-1 on residuals (strong directional autocorr). The trader buys the dip → market keeps falling → quote pinned at the cap. The post-mortem in `390764.py`'s docstring captured this exactly.

### 390764 — trader4 (regression-fix, **−7207**)

- **Intent**: roll back HYDROGEL to small sizing + take leg, keep liquid MM, add VEE range overlay.
- **Execution**:
  - HYDROGEL: 73 fills @ **−5.61 avg edge**, again pinned at +200 — the *take leg* alone bleeds because the EMA fair lags a momentum-driven price (AC-1 = 0.96). Buying when ask is below EMA is buying mid-down-trend.
  - VEE / liquid vouchers: same 0-fill pattern. Range overlay didn't trigger meaningful trades.
- **Verdict**: even a **take-only** HYDROGEL leg is unprofitable. The "rollback" wasn't far enough — needed to remove the take leg entirely, which is what trader5 did.

### 390796 — trader5 (passive hedging + VEE range overlay, +869)

- **Intent**: trader2 baseline preserved (HYDROGEL untouched), hedge with **limit orders** unless |delta| breaches a hard threshold, plus VEE range overlay biasing residuals toward the mean-reverting centre.
- **Execution**:
  - HYDROGEL: identical +627. (Same 9 fills @ +6.56 — the strategy code is byte-identical to trader2 on HYDROGEL.)
  - VEE: 15 fills @ **−0.37 avg edge** (a 5× cost reduction vs trader2's −2.08). VEE bleed cut from −449 → **−84**.
  - Liquid vouchers: 0 fills.
- **Verdict**: hypothesis validated — passive hedging saves spread cost. This is the first submission to confirm "VEE bleed is structural, fixable by avoiding market orders on the hedge".

### 390887 — trader7 (hedger disabled, +953)

- **Intent**: skip the hedger entirely; carry unhedged ~417-Δ long from VEV_4000+VEV_4500 against a low-drift VEE.
- **Execution**: VEE own trades = **0**. VEE PnL = **0**. HYDROGEL +627, voucher arb +325 → +953 cleanly.
- **Verdict**: "no hedge" is strictly better than "active hedge" on this single-day window. Saves the −84 spread cost from trader5 and the −449 from trader2. Comes with carried delta risk, but the day's VEE drift was small enough that it didn't matter. **This is the right intuition: in 5198–5300 range trading with 5-tick spread, every hedge round-trip costs more than the implied delta P&L**.

### 390935 — trader8 (passive VEE MM around EMA, **+2196**)

- **Intent**: keep hedger off; with the freed VEE inventory budget, post symmetric passive quotes around the slow VEE EMA at narrow margin, with tight inventory clearing.
- **Execution**: 33 VEE own trades @ **+1.26 avg edge** (positive! VEE MM successfully captured the spread instead of paying it). VEE PnL **+1243** — by far the largest single-product win in any official run.
- **Verdict**: this is the breakthrough. Treating VEE not as a hedge instrument but as a standalone MM target turns a chronic loss centre into the biggest profit centre. Tight EMA tracking + small inventory cap kept aggregate option delta exposure manageable without explicit hedging.

### 390966 — trader9 (trader8 + liquid voucher MM, +2196)

- **Intent**: keep trader8's VEE MM, re-enable liquid voucher MM on {5200, 5300, 5400, 5500} at 10% sizing, half-spread = max(½·spread_ema, 1).
- **Execution**: **identical to trader8 across every product.** Liquid MM block placed quotes (per code path inspection) but **0 fills on all four target strikes** for the entire day.
  - The likely cause: when the strategy uses live IV inversion (which succeeded on 100% of voucher rows in the analysis), `BlackScholes.call_price(S, K, T, iv_used) ≡ option_mid` by construction. The MM block then quotes at `mid ± max(½·spread_ema, 1)` — i.e. at most 1 tick inside the natural spread. The natural spread on liquid strikes is roughly the same width as the half-spread, so the bot's quotes sit at the touch but never further inside, and no aggressive counterparty crossed in this 1000-tick window.
- **Verdict**: the liquid-MM hypothesis (vouchers are mispriced enough to MM) is **untested**, not falsified. The block ran, but with a quote model that collapses to "join the mid" whenever IV inverts. Before re-trying, the model needs to use a *different* fair value than the option's own mid.

## 7. Patterns Worth Keeping

1. **HYDROGEL_PACK at small-size passive MM only.** 9 fills × +6.56 avg edge = +627, regardless of which surrounding strategy is wrapped around it. Every "improvement" attempt at full size lost ≥6× this gain. **Do not touch it.**
2. **Deep-ITM intrinsic arb on VEV_4000 / VEV_4500.** A combined +325 PnL in every run, code path is identical. Reliable, low-noise.
3. **Skip the active hedger.** trader7 (+953) > trader5 (+869) > trader2 (+503) tracks the progression: less hedging is more PnL on this day's VEE volatility.
4. **VEE as a passive MM target around a slow EMA.** trader8's +1243 VEE PnL is the single biggest win observed. Anchor on EMA, narrow margin, tight inventory caps.
5. **Treat IV inversion failure as a *signal*, not a fallback.** Deep-ITM voucher arb works precisely because `implied_vol` returns `None` (option mid below intrinsic) — the trader can take the no-arb edge. When inversion succeeds, the BS model is informationally trivial.

## Patterns to Avoid

1. **HYDROGEL full-size MM or take leg.** AC-1 = 0.96 means residuals trend; any take leg buys mid-trend. trader3 / trader4 lost ~6.5–7k each from this single mistake.
2. **Active (market-order) delta hedging on VEE.** 5-tick spread × 60+ chase trades = ~−450. Every hedge round-trip on VEE is net-negative versus carrying the delta.
3. **Reducing hedge sizing without re-tuning sizing-coupled blocks.** trader2-with-0.1×-hedger underperformed full-size-hedger trader2 because the residual MM block was now placing quotes against partially-hedged exposure.
4. **MM blocks that quote symmetrically around the option's own mid.** When IV inversion succeeds, BS-fair == mid, so the quotes collapse onto the natural touch and never get filled. trader3/trader4/trader9 all suffered this.
5. **Submitting visually-similar code as "duplicates" without changelog.** 389010 ≠ 389093 and 390935 ≠ 390966; the diffs were buried in mid-method tweaks that took targeted greps to surface. This makes post-hoc analysis significantly harder.

## 8. Recommended Iteration

### Immediate (cheap, high-value)

- **Add lambdaLog telemetry** to every trader version. At minimum: per-tick `{ product: { live_iv, iv_used, fair, quoted_bid, quoted_ask, position } }`. Without this we are guessing why VEV_5200/5300/5400/5500 never filled. Cost: a few `print()` calls; benefit: every future post-mortem becomes 10× faster and more conclusive.
- **Enforce a strict naming convention for submissions.** `trader{N}_v{m}.py` matching the docstring header, with a one-line PR-style changelog at the top. Renaming `390966.py` → `trader9.py` retroactively is fine.

### Voucher-MM (the unsolved problem)

The core defect is that BS-fair = option mid whenever IV inverts. To get fills on liquid strikes we need a fair value that **differs** from the market mid in a structurally informed way:

1. **Smile-based fair**: compute live IV per strike, fit a quadratic smile across strikes (or just a robust per-tick mean), then re-price each strike from the smile-implied IV instead of its own IV. Mispriced strikes will deviate from the smile fit.
2. **Term-structure / RV-based fair**: EDA report shows realized vol ≈ 0.41 vs mean IV ≈ 0.25 → vouchers may be systematically underpriced. Use a long-window RV estimate as the IV input, scaled by a confidence factor. (trader3's "vol_tilt" attempted this and blew up via forced hedging — the lesson is to trade the *long-vol* signal without tightly coupling to a market-order hedger.)
3. **Quote inside the touch by ≥ 1 tick** instead of at it. Even a 2-tick narrower quote should generate fills if there's any directional flow on these strikes.

### VEE / hedging

- **Test trader8 with multi-day backtests before re-introducing any hedging.** The 1-day window may have been kind to "no hedge"; a +5σ VEE move on a 5-day expiry would punish unhedged 417-Δ exposure heavily. Run `tools/run_backtest.py --round 3 --trader round3/strats/trader8.py` continuously across all available days and look at *worst-day* PnL, not mean.
- **If hedging is needed, do it via VEE limit-order placement only**, with size scaled by *expected* unhedged drift (proportional to remaining TTE × |delta|), not the instantaneous mismatch.

### Quality & robustness

- **Edge-vs-mid is the leading indicator.** Set a guardrail in CI/local-backtest: any product with avg_edge ≤ 0 across ≥ 30 fills should fail the run. trader3/trader4 would have flagged immediately on HYDROGEL at −6.66 / −5.61.
- **Position-limit pinning is a guardrail.** Any trader that hits the position cap should log the event; sustained pinning (>5% of ticks) should auto-pause the offending product.
- **Strategy-version gating**: add a 1-line strategy ID to `traderData` so the post-hoc analyzer can confirm which version actually ran (independent of the .py snapshot).

### Roadmap (priorities)

1. Add telemetry → re-run trader9 → confirm whether liquid MM quotes are placed at the touch or further out. *(2 hours.)*
2. Build smile-based fair value, test in `local-` backtest → submit as trader10 only if it MM-fills > 20 voucher trades / day. *(1 day.)*
3. Multi-day stress test trader8 → if PnL distribution is non-negative on every day, lock it in as the round-3 final candidate. *(1 day.)*
4. Re-evaluate VEE range-trading (trader5's overlay) using *only* the residual-MM channel, no hedger at all. *(0.5 day.)*
